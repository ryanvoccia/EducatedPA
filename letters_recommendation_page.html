Letters of Recommendation: How to Ask and What Makes Them Strong

Published on June 28, 2025 ‚Ä¢ 7 min read

Letters of recommendation can make or break your PA school application. While you can't control what your recommenders write, you can significantly influence the quality of your letters through strategic selection of recommenders, thoughtful preparation, and professional relationship building.

Strong letters of recommendation do more than confirm your academic abilities‚Äîthey provide insight into your character, work ethic, and potential as a future healthcare provider that no other part of your application can offer.

Understanding What Programs Want

The Purpose of Recommendation Letters

PA programs use letters of recommendation to gain insight into:

  • Professional behavior in healthcare settings
  • Interpersonal skills with patients and colleagues
  • Growth potential and ability to handle feedback
  • Character traits that predict success in healthcare
  • Specific examples of your skills and abilities in action

Types of Recommenders Programs Value Most

Healthcare Professionals (Most Important)

  • Physician Assistants - Can speak directly to your fit for the profession
  • Physicians - Understand the collaborative PA-physician relationship
  • Nurse Practitioners - Provide perspective on advanced practice roles
  • Other Healthcare Providers - Physical therapists, pharmacists, etc.

Direct Supervisors

  • Managers from patient care experience roles
  • Clinical supervisors who've observed your work
  • Healthcare facility administrators who know your contributions

Academic References

  • Science professors who can attest to your academic abilities
  • Professors who've seen your intellectual curiosity and work ethic
  • Research mentors who've guided your projects

Who Should You Ask?

Ideal Recommender Profile

The best recommenders combine several key characteristics:

  • Know you well professionally - Can provide specific examples
  • Have relevant credentials - Healthcare background preferred
  • Can write effectively - Clear, compelling communication skills
  • Support your goals - Enthusiastic about your PA school aspirations
  • Are reliable - Will submit letters on time

Strategic Selection

Most programs require 3-4 letters. Consider this ideal combination:

  • Letter 1: PA or physician you've worked with closely
  • Letter 2: Direct supervisor from patient care experience
  • Letter 3: Science professor or academic mentor
  • Letter 4: Additional healthcare professional or supervisor

Building Relationships Before You Need Them

In Healthcare Settings

Be Proactive

  • Introduce yourself to PAs and physicians during clinical rotations
  • Ask thoughtful questions about their experiences
  • Show genuine interest in learning from them
  • Follow up on advice they give you

Demonstrate Your Best Qualities

  • Arrive early and stay late when appropriate
  • Take initiative in patient care tasks
  • Show compassion and professionalism with patients
  • Be reliable and consistent in your performance

In Academic Settings

Engage Meaningfully

  • Participate actively in class discussions
  • Visit office hours to discuss course material
  • Seek research opportunities with faculty
  • Show intellectual curiosity beyond required coursework

How to Ask for a Letter

Timing Your Request

  • Ask early - At least 6-8 weeks before deadline
  • Confirm availability - Ensure they can meet your timeline
  • Be flexible - Work around their schedule when possible
  • Follow up appropriately - Gentle reminders as needed

The Initial Request

Whether in person, by phone, or email, your request should include:

Email Template:

Subject: Letter of Recommendation Request for PA School

Dear Dr. Smith,

I hope this email finds you well. I am writing to ask if you would be willing to write a letter of recommendation for my PA school applications. The deadline for submission is [DATE].

As you know, I have been working as a medical assistant in your clinic for the past two years, and your guidance has been instrumental in my decision to pursue the PA profession. I believe you can speak to my clinical skills, work ethic, and interactions with patients and staff.

I would be happy to provide you with my personal statement, resume, and any other materials that would be helpful. Would you be available to discuss this in person or over the phone?

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely, [Your Name]

Supporting Your Recommenders

Essential Information to Provide

Once someone agrees to write for you, provide a comprehensive packet including:

  • Personal statement draft - Shows your goals and motivations
  • Resume or CV - Complete picture of your experiences
  • Transcript - Academic performance context
  • Program list - Where you're applying and why
  • Key accomplishments - Specific examples they might mention
  • Deadline reminders - Clear timeline expectations

The Letter of Recommendation Packet

Create a professional folder or email with:

  • Cover letter - Thanking them and explaining contents
  • Timeline - Important dates clearly marked
  • Submission instructions - CASPA process or direct submission
  • Contact information - For questions or follow-up
  • Talking points - Suggested areas they might address

What Makes a Letter Strong

Content Elements

Specific Examples

  • Concrete situations where you demonstrated key qualities
  • Measurable achievements or improvements
  • Comparison to other students or employees
  • Evidence of growth and learning

Professional Qualities

  • Clinical skills and medical knowledge
  • Communication abilities with patients and colleagues
  • Reliability and work ethic
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking
  • Leadership potential and teamwork

Personal Characteristics

  • Compassion and empathy
  • Integrity and ethical behavior
  • Resilience and stress management
  • Commitment to learning and improvement

Format and Structure

Strong letters typically follow this structure:

  • Introduction - Relationship to applicant and context
  • Body paragraphs - Specific examples and qualities
  • Comparison - How you compare to others
  • Conclusion - Strong endorsement and confidence

Red Flags in Letters

Warning Signs of Weak Letters

  • Generic content - Could apply to anyone
  • Lack of specific examples - Vague praise without evidence
  • Lukewarm endorsement - "Good" instead of "excellent"
  • Focus on potential rather than performance - Suggests limited experience
  • Damning with faint praise - Subtle negative indicators

How to Avoid Weak Letters

  • Choose recommenders who know you well
  • Give plenty of advance notice
  • Provide comprehensive supporting materials
  • Follow up appropriately but not excessively
  • Consider asking for feedback on letter drafts if appropriate

Managing the Process

Organization Systems

Keep track of your recommendation letters with:

  • Spreadsheet tracker - Names, deadlines, submission status
  • Calendar reminders - Follow-up dates and deadlines
  • Email folders - Organized correspondence
  • Backup plans - Additional recommenders if needed

Following Up Professionally

Timeline for Follow-ups:

  • 2 weeks before deadline - Gentle reminder
  • 1 week before deadline - Urgent but polite follow-up
  • 2-3 days before deadline - Final reminder with backup plan

Special Circumstances

Limited Healthcare Experience

If you have minimal healthcare exposure:

  • Focus on volunteer supervisors who've seen your commitment
  • Ask professors who can speak to your scientific aptitude
  • Include employers who've observed your work ethic and reliability
  • Consider research mentors who know your analytical skills

Career Changers

For non-traditional applicants:

  • Include supervisors from your previous career
  • Focus on transferable skills and qualities
  • Emphasize any healthcare-related volunteering or experience
  • Show progression toward your PA goals

Recent Graduates

If you're fresh out of college:

  • Prioritize professors from science courses
  • Include research advisors or lab supervisors
  • Ask supervisors from any healthcare volunteering
  • Consider academic advisors who know your goals

After Submission

Expressing Gratitude

  • Thank you notes - Send immediately after submission
  • Update on outcomes - Share interview invitations and acceptances
  • Future correspondence - Maintain professional relationships
  • Small tokens of appreciation - Appropriate gifts if desired

Maintaining Relationships

Your recommenders can become:

  • Professional mentors throughout PA school
  • References for residency or job applications
  • Networking connections in your career
  • Colleagues in your future practice

Conclusion

Strong letters of recommendation are the result of deliberate relationship building, strategic selection, and thoughtful preparation. They require time, effort, and genuine professional connections‚Äîbut they can be the factor that sets your application apart from the competition.

Remember that your recommenders are investing their time and reputation in supporting your goals. Treat the process with the professionalism and respect it deserves, and you'll not only secure strong letters but also build lasting relationships that will benefit you throughout your healthcare career.

Start building these relationships early, be strategic in your selections, and support your recommenders with everything they need to write compelling letters. Your future as a physician assistant may well depend on the strength of these professional endorsements.
